

# Landing Page Marketing Audit -- CampusVoice.AI

## Overview

After reviewing the main landing page (`/`) and the agency page (`/for-agencies`) as a prospective ICP (CMO, VP Comms, agency lead in higher ed), here are the issues and improvement opportunities organized by impact.

---

## High-Impact Issues

### 1. No Navigation Bar on the Main Landing Page
The main landing page has no persistent nav. There is no way to reach the Agency page, pricing info, or any context about the company without scrolling to the very bottom footer. A first-time visitor has no orientation. The Agency page *does* have a proper header nav -- the main page should match.

**Recommendation:** Add a lightweight top nav to the landing page with: Logo, For Universities (active), For Agencies, Sign In, Request Access CTA.

### 2. Hero Lacks Specificity -- "Built for Writers" is Confusing
The rotating noun ("Writers", "Marketers", "Strategists", etc.) cycles through too many personas. A CMO landing here sees "Built for Writers" and may bounce -- it does not signal strategic value. The subtitle "Strategic Messaging Intelligence" is buried below and does not land fast enough.

**Recommendation:** Lead with the value prop, not the persona. Something like: "Your Brand. Every Channel. Always On-Voice." with "Strategic Messaging Intelligence" as the hero badge, not buried subtext. Keep the rotating noun as a secondary element, not the headline.

### 3. No Social Proof Anywhere
Neither page has testimonials, logos, case studies, user counts, or any third-party validation. The "Brand protection at scale" section has a single quote but it reads as fabricated (no attribution, no photo, no institution name). For a B2B SaaS targeting enterprise education, this is a significant trust gap.

**Recommendation:** Add a "Trusted by" logo bar (even if it is "Early partners include..." with 3-5 logos). Add at least 2-3 attributed testimonials with name, title, and institution.

### 4. No Pricing Signal
There is zero mention of pricing on either page. B2B buyers in higher ed need to know if this is self-serve, enterprise-only, or usage-based before they will fill out a form. The agency page mentions "custom pricing" but offers no anchor.

**Recommendation:** Add a simple pricing section or at minimum a "Starting at..." signal, or "Free pilot for qualifying institutions" to reduce friction.

### 5. CTA Friction -- "Join the Beta" vs "Request Access" vs "Become a Partner"
The CTAs are inconsistent across pages. The main page says "Join the Beta" (top) but then "Request Beta Access" (bottom). The sticky bar says "Request Access". The agency page says "Become a Partner" and "Apply Now". This creates confusion about what stage the product is in and what the user is signing up for.

**Recommendation:** Unify CTAs. If you are in beta, own it clearly: "Get Early Access" everywhere. If you are past beta, drop the beta language entirely.

---

## Medium-Impact Issues

### 6. Product Tour Section is Very Long
The 5 showcases (Message Builder, Journey Builder, Image Studio, Brand Studio, AI Copywriter) create a very long scroll. Most visitors will not reach the bottom. The value prop section ("Stop Reacting. Start Planning.") and the features grid are buried below 5 product demos.

**Recommendation:** Reduce to 3 showcases max above the fold break. Move the remaining 2 into a tabbed or carousel format. Elevate the "Stop Reacting. Start Planning." section higher on the page.

### 7. "How It Works" Section Missing Context
I did not see a clear "How It Works" section with numbered steps on the main page (it exists on the agency page). First-time visitors need a 3-step mental model: "Upload your brand > Generate content > Govern at scale" or similar.

**Recommendation:** Add a concise 3-step "How It Works" above the product tour.

### 8. Footer is Minimal
The footer has only a logo, copyright, and a contact email. No links to features, about, privacy policy, terms, or social media. This signals an early/incomplete product.

**Recommendation:** Add a proper footer with column links: Product, Company, Legal, Social.

### 9. Agency Page Mock Dashboard is Static
The client portfolio mock on the agency page is impressive but lists 24 real university names (Ohio State, Michigan, etc.) which could be misleading -- it implies these are actual clients. 

**Recommendation:** Use fictional/generic institution names or clearly label it as "Example Dashboard" with a visual indicator.

---

## Low-Impact / Polish

### 10. Mobile Responsiveness
The "Plan | Strategize | Execute" pills in the hero may be hard to tap on smaller screens. The sticky CTA bar competes with the Beta Feedback button on mobile.

### 11. "Powered by Persuasion Science" Section
This section heading is intriguing but the cards below it are generic feature descriptions, not actual science. Either cite real research or rename this section.

### 12. No "For Agencies" Link from Main Page
Unless a visitor knows the URL, they cannot discover the agency offering. Add it to the nav and/or footer.

---

## Summary of Recommended Implementation Priority

1. Add top navigation bar to landing page (with For Agencies link)
2. Rework hero headline to lead with value, not persona rotation
3. Add social proof section (logos + testimonials)
4. Unify CTA language across all pages
5. Add pricing signal or free pilot callout
6. Condense product tour from 5 to 3 showcases
7. Add "How It Works" 3-step section to main landing page
8. Expand footer with proper link columns
9. Rename agency mock universities to fictional names
